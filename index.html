FAQ & Safety Rules Policy

Review the community code of conduct, trail protocols, and altitude safety protocols.

1. What is the trail safety policy?

Always follow the team guide/sherpa. Keep to the mountain side when pack animals are passing. Carry a basic medical card detailing emergency contacts and emergency backup info.

2. What should I do in case of altitude sickness?

Inform your team leader Sundar Gurung or Pasang Sherpa immediately. Descend immediately if symptoms (headache, dizziness, nausea) worsen. Oxygen logs are managed at every camp stop.

3. How does deposit verification work?

Submit your registration via our Participation Form. Go to the Payment Info tab, scan our QR, and upload your payment transfer screenshot. Admins will verify the screenshot in the Reconciliation Console.

Trek Preparedness Guide

A comprehensive gear log and physical guidelines to prepare for the Himalayas.

Gear Checklist

  • Thermal body layers
  • Waterproof windbreakers
  • Sturdy trekking boots (broken-in)
  • Warm sleeping bag (-10°C)
  • Polarized sunglasses & UV cream

Fitness Guidelines

We recommend cards representing 5-8km of walking with 10kg loaded backpacks twice a week starting 1 month before your departure. Cardio workouts greatly aid lung capacity for high altitude base camps.
